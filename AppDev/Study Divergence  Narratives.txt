Following a series of manual stress tests (Audit 0817-36), we have identified a significant "Competitive Gap" between static branching species and mobile foraging species. This version (1.2) implements a balancing pass to ensure multi-species coexistence under catastrophic stress.Findings from Audit 0817-361. Competitive Hegemony (The "Tree" Problem)Observation: The Tree lineage consistently dominated the population ratio (98:2).Cause: The Tree's growth was too "cheap" and too frequent, allowing it to carpet the world and starve the mobile swarm agents before they could forage.Adjustment: Nerfed Tree branching probability ($0.022 \to 0.015$) and increased per-node energy cost ($0.0025 \to 0.0040$).2. The Graze-Death SpiralObservation: Swarm agents often starved immediately upon spawning during recovery phases.Cause: High "Graze" consumption created local energy voids faster than the environment could replenish them.Adjustment: Reduced "Linger" consumption rate ($0.015 \to 0.012$) and increased spawn probability ($0.006 \to 0.012$).3. Bottleneck Recovery (Cataclysm Logic)Observation: "Phoenix Surge" recovery often failed because replenishment was too slow for the few remaining survivors.Adjustment: Refined the Cataclysm logic to leave a trace residual energy pulse (0.02) to give mutants a "starting meal" before total starvation.Updated Scenarios (v1.2)Scenario 4: The Phoenix Surge (Balanced)Focus: Mutant recovery after total wipe.Trigger: Click Trigger Cataclysm.Strategy: High Jump Mutation + Low Replenish.Expected Outcome: The Lineage Map should now show both Jade and Amber lines surviving the bottleneck, competing to recolonize the void.ConclusionThe Convergence Lab now exhibits a more balanced "Arms Race."
