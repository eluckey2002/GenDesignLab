# The Cross-Dimension Hypothesis: A "Rosetta Stone" for Design

The **Cross-Dimension Hypothesis** is the core theory of this framework. It suggests that the "science" of how things grow, move, and survive isn't locked into biology. Instead, by breaking a system into **Pattern-makers, Worlds, and Pressures**, we can discover universal "Interaction Effects" that apply to everything from architecture to economics.

---

## 1. The Core Premise: Portability as Proof

The hypothesis states that if a behavioral rule is truly "foundational," it should be **portable**

- We proved this by taking **Metabolism** (the `EnergyField` module) out of a slow-growing vine (**Slow Frill**) and plugging it into a chaotic "foraging" insect (**Wake**)
- Even though the "actors" changed, the **Metabolism** worked exactly the same way—proving that "Energy Scarcity" is a universal law that dictates behavior regardless of the context

## 3. The Unified Theory: Modeling Nature in a Sandbox

The hypothesis argues that there is a **Commonality Path** for all bodies of knowledge [cite: 59]. Whether you are a designer, a researcher, or an AI agent, the framework allows you to ask the same powerful question:

> _"What happens when this pattern-maker lives in this world under this pressure?"_

### Success Metrics for the Sandbox:

- **Growth Strategy:** The ratio of form built to energy consumed (Efficiency).
- **Extinction:** The threshold where operating costs exceed the world's yield (Failure) [cite: 49].
- **Resilience:** The "Time to Baseline" for a world to recover after depletion [cite: 49, 56].
- **Phylogeny:** The branching rate showing how a design "pivots" under stress.
