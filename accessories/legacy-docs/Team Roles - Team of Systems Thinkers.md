To execute this framework effectively, your team needs a mix of System Architects, Computational Designers, and Domain Specialists. You aren’t just looking for "artists"; you are looking for people who can build, break, and measure complex digital ecosystems.Based on the requirements of the framework, here are the essential skillsets:1. The System Architect (The Engine Builder)This person builds the "portable" infrastructure. They ensure that modules like Metabolism can be plugged into any new project without breaking. Key Skill: Modular Software Engineering. They must understand how to separate the "Pattern-maker" from the "World" so that behaviors are not trapped inside a single file. Role in the Sandbox: Implementing the Stochastic Linger Logic for foragers and ensuring the EnergyField remains a single "source of truth" for all competing actors.2. The Computational Designer (The Pattern-maker)This person is responsible for the Growth and Foraging strategies. They don't draw shapes; they write the rules that create them. Key Skill: Algorithm Design (Biomimicry). They should be familiar with Differential Growth (Frill Logic) and Strange Attractors (Scouts). Role in the Sandbox: Tuning the k_consume and substep rates to prevent immediate Extinction while maximizing the visual complexity of the design.3. The Data Storyteller / Researcher (The Readout Specialist)This person manages the Lineage and Phylogeny tools. They translate the "math" into "meaning". Key Skill: Data Visualization & Analysis. They need to be able to look at a heatmap or a family tree and identify the "Interaction Effects" between form and pressure. Role in the Sandbox: Setting the Success Metrics and reporting on Resilience—identifying exactly when a system is "Healthy" (Mean E: 0.85–0.95) versus "Parasitic".4. The Domain Specialist (The Industry Expert)Because this framework is a "Rosetta Stone," you need someone who knows the "World" you are building for. Key Skill: Industry Knowledge (e.g., Architecture, Urban Planning, or Material Science). Role in the Sandbox: Providing the Pressures that matter. For an architect, this is "Sunlight and Heat"; for an urban planner, it is "Crowd Flow and Bottlenecks". The Team "Commonality Path"The most important trait across all these roles is Systems Thinking. Every team member must understand that the primary goal is not to make an isolated visual, but to create a reusable behavior that adds to the Phylogeny of Design.
